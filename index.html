Shubdeep Mohapatra

PhD Candidate, Computer Engineering

North Carolina State University

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Computer Engineering at North Carolina State University, advised by Dr. Huiyang Zhou and Dr. Yuan Liu. My research is focused around error analysis and mitigation in quantum circuits and the relatively new quantum computing architecture involving Hybrid Continuous Variable–Discrete Variable (CV-DV) systems. Specifically, I aim to find the vulnerable regions in a quantum circuit when it is subjected to rotation errors, helping pinpoint the locations most susceptible to errors and selectively protect them. My most recent work in this area aims to find the best circuit reliability metric to quantify the error experienced by a circuit—important because many error mitigation schemes that use extrapolation to the zero-noise case require an accurate quantification of circuit error.

Hybrid CV-DV quantum computing combines traditional discrete-variable qubits with continuous-variable bosonic modes (qumodes), leveraging the infinite-dimensional Hilbert space of quantum harmonic oscillators to achieve significant resource advantages for tasks like quantum simulation and error correction. On this front, I have developed HyQBench, an open-source benchmarking suite for Hybrid CV-DV quantum circuits built on Bosonic Qiskit. I am currently working on noise modeling for these hybrid systems, studying how errors such as photon loss and dephasing affect CV-DV applications. I have also recently released HyQSim, a browser-based visual simulator for hybrid CV-DV circuits that allows researchers to build, simulate, and visualize these systems interactively.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my undergraduate degree with a dual major in Physics and Computer Science at Birla Institute of Technology and Sciences (BITS) Pilani.

Research

Quantum Error Analysis

Investigating error patterns in quantum circuits, with a focus on identifying vulnerable zones susceptible to rotation errors. This work informs targeted error mitigation strategies by systematically testing circuits under realistic noise models. In our recent work, Benchmarking Fidelity Metrics of Quantum Computers, we evaluate how different fidelity measurement approaches correlate with Density Matrix Fidelity across various quantum hardware platforms and apply these findings to improve extrapolation-based error mitigation schemes.

Hybrid CV-DV Systems

Studying hybrid Continuous Variable–Discrete Variable quantum computing architectures. This hybrid approach drastically cuts overhead for tasks like Shor's algorithm and simulation of physical systems containing mixed bosonic and fermionic components. We built HyQSim, a browser-based visual simulator for hybrid CV-DV circuits featuring drag-and-drop circuit construction, real-time Wigner function and Bloch sphere visualization, and dual backends (in-browser JS and Python/bosonic-qiskit).

Try HyQSim →

Benchmarking Suite Development

Building HyQBench, an open-source benchmarking suite for hybrid CV-DV quantum circuits. It spans eight benchmarks—from primitives like state transfer and cat state generation to applications like Hamiltonian simulation and Shor's algorithm—with CV-DV–specific metrics including Wigner negativity, truncation cost, and energy.
